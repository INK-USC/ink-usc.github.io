We introduce entity triggers, an effective proxy of human explanaations for facilitating label-efficient learning of NER models. We crowd-sourced 14k entity triggers for two well-studied NER datasets.

  1. Trigger Dataset: We introduce the concept of “entity triggers,” a novel form of explanatory annotation for named entity recognition problems. We crowd-source and publicly release 14k annotated entity triggers on two popular datasets: CoNLL03 (generic domain), BC5CDR (biomedical domain).

  2. Novel learning framework: We propose a novel learning framework, named Trigger Matching Network, which encodes entity triggers and softly grounds them on unlabeled sentences to increase the effectiveness of the base entity tagger

  3. NER Performance Improvement: Experimental results show that the proposed trigger-based framework is significantly more cost-effective. The TMN uses 20% of the trigger-annotated sentences from the original CoNLL03 dataset, while achieving a comparable performance to the conventional model using 70% of the annotated sentences.

Trigger Matching Networks

Trigger Matching Networks (TMN) has two-stage of training and inference:

  • Training: We first jointly train the Trigger Encoder (via trigger classification) and the Trigger Matcher (via contrastive loss). Then, we reuse the training data trigger vectors as attention queries in the Sequence Tagger.

  • Inference: It uses the Trigger Matcher to retrieve the k nearest triggers and average their trigger vectors as the attention query for the trained Sequence Tagger. Thus, an unseen cue phrase (e.g., “the president of”) can be matched with a seen trigger (e.g., “the leader of”).
